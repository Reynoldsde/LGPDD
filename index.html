Our Success Stories

DRF Grant Impact in 2024

Through the DRF grant to LGPD in 2024, these key project impacts have been highlighted by beneficiaries

M

N. Monique

Mother of three children, woman with physical disability

Before joining LGPD: Monique wasn't aware of her rights and felt afraid due to hurtful treatment from her family and society. She felt ashamed and upset about her disability, recalling how neighbours had mistreated her. She didn't feel valuable.

After UNCRPD trainings funded by DRF: She learned that she, too, had rights and was capable. LGPD helped her realize her worth. Through the trainings, Monique gained new skills about the rights of PWDs and feels confident.

Transformation: She now participates in community work monthly, participates in decision-making, advocating for children and girls with disabilities. She works with local authorities to challenge negative societal perceptions. "Tomorrow is brighter, because she is capable, too."

F

Francine

Blind woman, massage therapist

Before UNCRPD training: Francine felt hopeless, and society, as well as family, tortured her due to her blindness, which made her life hampered. She couldn't see a future for herself.

After training sessions: Her life quickly changed. She began to feel capable and empowered to improve her life situation. With determination, she worked hard to create job opportunities for herself.

Achievement: She enrolled in massage courses and now practices kinesiotherapy to support her family. She encourages others: "disability is not inability" and advocates for hiring people with disabilities.

C

Chantal

Mother of Benita, girl with physical disability

Before training: Chantal felt ashamed of her daughter's disability and faced mockery from family and neighbors. She struggled to accept and support her daughter's needs.

After learning about UNCRPD: She learned about the rights of people with disabilities, especially children, and embraced her daughter's needs with full love and acceptance.

Victory: Chantal took charge of her daughter's health, reached out to healthcare providers. Benita received surgery, is now healed, walking confidently, and attending school with other children.

C

Celestin

Father of Patrick, child with clubfoot

Initial struggle: Celestin admits it was difficult at first to accept having a child with a disability. Before learning about LGPD, he felt ashamed of his son and struggled with negative comments from others.

After UNCRPD training: He gained the knowledge and confidence to love his child fully. Through LGPD's advocacy and collaboration with other disability organizations, he was encouraged to seek further medical treatment.

Current impact: Patrick is receiving surgery at Rilima Hospital. Celestin now actively encourages other parents not to isolate their children or feel ashamed. He believes children with disabilities are just as capable and deserving of love.

Gratitude & Impact

"All beneficiaries express deep gratitude to DRF for funding LGPD to empower them through understanding the rights of people with disabilities. The training has helped them love themselves, build confidence, and envision a brighter future."

✨ These transformations showcase the profound impact of rights-based education and community support in changing lives and breaking barriers.

About LGPD

A beacon of hope and empowerment in Rwanda

The Life Gate for Persons with Disabilities (LGPD) is more than an organization—we are a movement of hope, empowerment, and transformation. Founded in 2020 by 11 dedicated advocates, we believe that every person, regardless of their abilities, deserves dignity, respect, and the opportunity to thrive.
